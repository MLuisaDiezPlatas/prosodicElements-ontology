postdata-prosodicElements ontology: Description back to ToC

As mentioned in previously sections, the objective of this ontology is to cover the aspects relating to the prosodic analysis of a poem. This ontology contains the classes and properties necessary to structure the information of a prosodic analysis, that is to say, the information obtained from the study of the meters and the forms of versification. In poetry, the metric is the basic rhythmic structure of the verses of a poetic work.

The classes defined in ontology focus primarily on handling of metric patterns using the class LinePattern, StanzaPattern and WorkPattern. There is also a class for modelling rhymes and another Metaplasm class for processing and storing information about a figure of transformation or metaplasm is a figure of diction that consists of altering the writing or pronunciation of words without altering their meaning.
Some examples are dieresis, syneresis, synalepha or hiatus.

The ontology prefix is pdprosodic

We have taken into account are the possibility to contemplate all the information necessary for prosodic analysis. In this sense, the following classes, among others, are defined:
  • The Rhyme class to represent the repetition of similar sounding words occurring at the end of lines in poems or songs .
  • The Foot class to represent a unit of poetic meter in most Indo-European poetic traditions, including the English syllabic verse and the quantitative compass of Greek and ancient Latin classical poetry. However, the metric syllable as metric unit in other traditions such as Spanish poetry is also contemplate.
  • The MetricalEncoding class to define the notation employed to represent a metrical pattern, for example, the plus sign to encode strong positions metric coding.

In the ontology the figures of transformation or metaplasms such as, for example, dieresis, syneresis, sinalefa or hiatus are also contemplated among other prosodic characteristics. These figures consist of altering the writing or pronunciation of words without altering their meaning.
The pdprosodic:Metaplasm class definition allows to represent the transformation figures detected during the prosodic analysis. The properties defined in this class are the necessary to represent the line in which is presented the metaplasm through pdprosodic:successIn property, the first of the words affected by a metaplasm through pdprosodic:affectsFirst property and the type of metaplasm for what has been defined pdprosodic:typeOfMetaplasm property whose range is a controlled vocabulary pdskos:metaplasm-type.

Use examples

In order to facilitate the understanding of the some classes and properties of this vocabulary, some examples are provided below.

Example 1. Work and Line Patterns. Rhyme and Syllabic Metrical Scheme

The example presented corresponds to the information about the prosody of the PoeticWork "MMais nos faz Santa María a séu Fillo perdõar ". The information is extracted from http://www.cantigasdesantamaria.com/

Example description

In the code, an example of the use of pdprosodic:WorkPattern, pdprosodic:LinePattern and pdprosodic:Rhyme classes is showed. Only one line is presented and the data as Creator and others is omitted to improve the legibility of the example. For the Rhyme class the pdprosodic:label and pdprosodic:ending properties has used. For the LinePattern class , pdprosodic: phoneticTranscription and pdprosodic: syllabicMetricalScheme properties are considered and for the WorkPattern class the properties that has been showed are pdprosodic:numberOfStanzas, pdprosodic: rhymeScheme and pdprosodic: syllabicMetricalScheme.

We will show the RDF codes generated with the data of this example. It should be noted that the instances will be represented by their URIs (Uniform Resource Identifier) created according to the following structure <http://postdata.linhd.uned.es/resource/{Class}/{UUID}>.
For example, the PoeticWork is identified by <http://postdata.linhd.uned.es/resource/PoeticWork/c41b20c6-7f10-11e9-ac29-f2189886299e>.
